\begin{abstract}
Kaladont is a word chain game from the former Yugoslav region in which each new word must begin with the last two letters of the previous one.
We investigate how well the game can be played across 33 languages from 15 language families.
For each language we build a word list from the English Wiktionary, filter out entries that would not be valid in the game (abbreviations, proper nouns, inflected forms, and — for well-covered languages — rare vocabulary), and run 1000 simulated games.
Average chain lengths range from 2 words (Greek) to 40 words (Serbo-Croatian, Latin script), with Georgian (34.9) and Serbo-Croatian in Cyrillic (29.6) also ranking highly.
South Slavic languages consistently outperform other families; Germanic languages average 4–5 words per game.
Interestingly, having a larger word list does not reliably lead to longer chains — what seems to matter more is how well word endings connect to word beginnings across the vocabulary.
\end{abstract}
