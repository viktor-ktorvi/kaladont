\section{Word Sample Quality Check}
\label{app:word-samples}

To give a sense of what the filtering pipeline keeps and what it discards, Table~\ref{tab:word-samples} shows a random sample of ten surviving words and ten filtered-out words for each language.
Surviving words are drawn from the final list used in simulation.
Filtered-out words are those that were present in the raw Wiktionary dump but removed by at least one filtering step: wrong part of speech, multi-word entry, inflected or alternative form, abbreviation, missing vowel, incorrect casing, or below the frequency threshold.
The samples are random and will differ between runs of the script (\texttt{uv run python -m scripts.word\_samples}).

\section{Example Game Sequences}
\label{app:example-chains}

Table~\ref{tab:example-chains} shows five example game sequences per language, each between three and eight words long, drawn at random from Monte Carlo simulations.
The sequences illustrate how the overlap rule plays out in practice and give an intuition for why some languages produce longer sequences than others.
For Korean the overlap is one syllable block rather than two characters, which is reflected in the single-character transitions shown.
