\section{Conclusion}


\subsection{Limitations}

A few caveats are worth keeping in mind.
All word lists come from the English Wiktionary, which covers some languages much better than others — smaller or less internationally prominent languages have fewer entries, which may skew the results.
The word lists are also imperfect: unusual loanwords, archaic terms, and some inflected forms can slip through the filters.
The simulation does not account for the fact that real players only know a fraction of the dictionary, and it does not model strategy — in a real game, a smart player would try to end their word on a two-letter combination that is hard to continue, whereas here the next word is always chosen at random.
